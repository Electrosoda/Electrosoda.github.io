Background - A very early stage When I joined Netboom, the product was still in early development. Although there is strong cloud technology support, from the user experience perspective, it is still challenging to go through a complete process smoothly and unimpeded. At this time, Netboom has not yet reached the standard of commercial-grade products and cannot pass the market test. The difficulties How to enable users who are in contact with cloud games for the first time to understand the product quickly How to provide rich functionality while maintaining ease of use How to combine and use the different scene characteristics of computer and mobile games How to Balance Entertainment Experience and Profit

This is also out of the consideration of saving R&D expenses, and other methods such as user interviews will be considered after a complete user flow is available. Potential users of Netboom may be computer gamers familiar with major game platforms (Steam, Epic Game) or mobile phone users. They might use our products while in transit or waiting for food at a restaurant. They can share the game with friends at the party or lie on their sofa and play the game. They might want to play games they've already played on their phone, discover new ones, browse reviews, or share them with friends. Generally speaking, cloud games are still relatively unfamiliar to most groups, which is a fundamental prerequisite. Consistency and standards The menu icons are not intuitive and do not meet industry-standard expectations. Visibility of System Status There is no feedback from key presses. There are redundant buttons that do not work when clicked. Flexibility and Efficiency of Use Text input steps are cumbersome. Users cannot customise the keyboard layout. Aesthetic and minimalist design The desktop background has a lot of content, most of which have nothing to do with product functionality. Large blocks of text are cumbersome and unclear. UI design lacks unity. For example, the arrow keys are complicated and take up too much screen. Recognise, diagnose and recover from errors. The language of the error message has not been adjusted, and the current display is code information.

Continuously expandable menu system design Regarding the game interface, menu operations at the Netboom level are essential. Based on the characteristics of Cloud Game, Netboom can provide very rich functions. How to leave room for future updates while maintaining usability is a big challenge. I compared the advantages and disadvantages of the popular game menu interfaces on the market and the corresponding operation modes. I designed a clearer and easier-to-use menu structure more suitable for Netboom.
