Netboom

Netboom is a cloud gaming platform that brings the best PC games to your portable devices

My Role

  • Design the overall UX framework, from 0 to 1.
  • Based on experiments and research, I proposed the preset scheme of the controller mode. This function has shown an overwhelming advantage in the subsequent data statistics and has become the product's most
  • frequently used operation mode.
  • Design and plan the in-game button customisation system, expand product functions, and make it possible for the product to adapt flexibly to various types of games.
  • Standardise and revise the UX language, organise the development of multilingual versions, and better realise localisation.
  • Research, experiment, and participate in the development of global pricing strategies.
  • Responsible for listing and updating products on Play Store and Appstore.
  • After several iterations, the user feedback on the product has been dramatically improved from "difficult to use" in the early stage to almost no barriers to interaction/understanding in later evaluations.

Background - A very early stage

When I joined Netboom, the product was still in early development. Although there is strong cloud technology support, from the user experience perspective, it is still challenging to go through a complete process smoothly and unimpeded. At this time, Netboom has not yet reached the standard of commercial-grade products and cannot pass the market test.

The difficulties

  • How to enable users who are in contact with cloud games for the first time to understand the product quickly
  • How to provide rich functionality while maintaining ease of use
  • How to combine and use the different scene characteristics of computer and mobile games
  • How to Balance Entertainment Experience and Profit

Developing a Point of View

I adopted a heuristic evaluation method to sort out the process of the initial version to ensure that users can enter the game smoothly and perform basic operations. This is also out of the consideration of saving R&D expenses, and other methods such as user interviews will be considered after a complete user flow is available.

Potential users of Netboom may be computer gamers familiar with major game platforms (Steam, Epic Game) or mobile phone users. They might use our products while in transit or waiting for food at a restaurant. They can share the game with friends at the party or lie on their sofa and play the game. They might want to play games they've already played on their phone, discover new ones, browse reviews, or share them with friends. Generally speaking, cloud games are still relatively unfamiliar to most groups, which is a fundamental prerequisite.

These are screenshots from an earlier version. First of all, the interface is very complicated and unclear, and second, the menu's functions are incomplete. The language doesn't precisely correspond to common sense, either. These would be very confusing for a new user who has no basics. The visual design is also relatively fragmented, with room for improvement in consistency and aesthetics.

To ensure the primary process first, I decided to focus on adjusting the keyboard layout settings for the above content. Based on the preset keyboard, a custom layout function is developed. I also Re-standardize the UI icon and design system to make it more uniform and improve readability.

Continuously expandable menu system design

Regarding the game interface, menu operations at the Netboom level are essential. Based on the characteristics of Cloud Game, Netboom can provide very rich functions. How to leave room for future updates while maintaining usability is a big challenge. I compared the advantages and disadvantages of the popular game menu interfaces on the market and the corresponding operation modes. I designed a clearer and easier-to-use menu structure more suitable for Netboom.

First Usability Test and Interviews

On the basis that the critical functions of the product have been improved, I applied for the first usability test. The testing aims to find out what is preventing the user from successfully playing the game. This test recruited 5 English speakers from different countries and races to our company in Shanghai. To get more inclusive information, the participants are both game novices and professional players. The gaming devices they have used include computers, consoles and mobile phones. The test had many unexpected results. The main problems that hinder entering the game are concentrated in the process guidance, the particularity of the mobile phone scene, and many other minor issues related to UX and UI.

Insights

  • Explanatory guide

    First-time product users often don't know how to do it without outside help. It took many attempts to complete the task or even fail to complete the job.

  • Mouse operation mode

    Users don't know how to move the mouse or be able to switch to touch mode, seriously hindering tasks.

  • The particularity of the mobile phone scene

    The complex keyboard operation mode of computer games is not suitable for the mobile scene. The implementation needs to be optimized.

  • UX Language & Other Problems

    The language and words do not conform to common sense habits and cannot adapt to the global market. UI consistency needs to be improved.

The Vision - Simplify and maintain consistency

Based on user research, I reorganised the main points of the product and formulated phased goals. Two main parts make up Netboom's product structure. The outer part is the App interface, and the inner part is after entering the game (the game interface). To ensure the integrity and ease of use of the primary process and allow users to operate independently, after discussions with the product team, development team and stakeholders, we decided to start with the App interface and gradually improve it to the game interface.

I conducted a comprehensive competitive analysis of cloud gaming products in the market, including international and Asian products. I try to grasp the strengths worth learning and the weaknesses to be aware of.

For example, how to position the overall UI style. Due to user habits and the historical accumulation of Internet development, Asian products tend to use icons + labels; while minimalist designs are more prevalent in European and American markets. However, the concept of cloud games is still relatively unfamiliar to most users. If it is too flattened, it will only be counterproductive. Some product homepages are flooded with subscriptions or advertisements, which is not desirable. Profit is important, but too much advertising is one factor that hinders users from continuing to learn about a brand-new product. There are still many points that we need to grasp and measure.

Another Round of User Interviews

For the second user test, we recruited participants in Shanghai and interviewed users in the United States online. This time the insights focus on the game interface, and it is worth mentioning that all participants’ comments on the homepage are positive.

  • Some essential functions require a more prominent entry. Most participants didn't notice the ability to switch between mouse and touch modes. And in fact, we provide a gamepad mode, and all participants do not know they can connect to a controller.
  • The gaming keyboard customisation system needs more concise and easy-to-understand menus and instructions. Most users don't know that it is possible to customise the keyboard.
  • Operations related to the typing keyboard need to be simplified. There are many steps to call up the keyboard, and the keyboard style will block the field of vision and other problems, which seriously affect the user's game progress.
  • Mouse mode needs to be considered again. The actual effect of the mouse mode is not good. The learning time is extended, the accuracy is also technically challenging, and it does not fit the mobile phone screen scene.

The keyboard in the game is a very tricky point. It is necessary to realise the rich functions of computer games on the mobile phone screen while ensuring ease of use.

  • After focusing on the button operation of dozens of popular games, we supplemented the preset button system and designed a new custom button system. Custom functions have been the focus of development in the subsequent iterations. Although it is a technical difficulty, it is also the key to improving the game experience.
  • Netboom works well in gamepad mode, which is beyond our expectations. I've noticed that many big games natively support controller mode, so why not use an already successful design? In addition to adding an entry prompt to the controller mode, after confirming with the technical team, I proposed to add a virtual controller button mode and design a separate icon entry for it in the menu. In this way, players without a controller can also experience the controller mode, which also coincides with Netboom's original intention to solve the lack of hardware devices for users. The virtual controller mode function has succeeded and even become the most used mode.
  • Touch mode takes precedence. We have mouse mode. You can control the mouse on the screen by dragging. We also have a touch mode similar to the touch on a normal mobile phone. Contrary to expectations, two rounds of user interviews and background data show that the touch mode is more suitable for mobile phone scenarios. I proposed making the touch mode the default and the mouse an optional feature. Within one month, we can see that the in-game usage time has increased significantly, while most users go with the touch mode, and very few users choose the mouse mode.
  • As for the typing keyboard, I found in my research that typing is a critical function that is frequently used. To do this, I reorganised the interactive flow of typing and gave it a separate button in the menu.

Respond to market feedback promptly, continuously upgrade and improve

After building the fundamental framework, I continued researching and updating the product with the team.

In addition to a complete UI and game features, it also includes updates to business strategies. For example, regarding game selection, the operation team and I researched and screened popular games more suitable for mobile phone scenarios and put these games on the product homepage. These games' click rates and duration are significantly higher than other games. I was involved in developing pricing schemes, such as subscription models and regular promotions, including extensive pricing testing.

Multilingual version

We soon discovered that the product performed well in the Southeast Asian market, and the number of new users in Thailand alone reached 990,000 in one month. The Middle East market has also brought us a lot of revenue growth. I proposed to provide multilingual versions to better adapt to the localised market. To this end, we recruited professional translators from various countries and invited users of our products to correct grammar and diction together. After we launched the different localised versions, many users chose to use their native language and received positive market feedback.

Develop the Middle East market

After we officially launched the product, I also participated in the daily work of operation teams while doing relative market research, such as implementing the customer service system and social media promotion. I started to be responsible for expanding the Middle East market. I focus on localisation and publicity in line with local preferences at the same time. The advertisement I am in charge of has achieved quite good results in countries in the Middle East. When Netboom entered the Turkish market for the first time, it reached a daily increase of one million users. At the same time, the budget is also controlled within a reasonable range, and the ROI can usually reach five or higher.

The combination key function is online.

After the foundation of the game interface is finished, I continued to study the direction of game interaction in depth. Key combinations are a very common function in games, whether launching special skills or switching items. Netboom has implemented a custom key layout to place any key on the phone screen. But putting fifteen function keys on a palm-sized screen is not suitable, and it is also challenging to press multiple keys simultaneously while holding the phone. With the joint efforts of the technical department, the combination key function has been launched. I worked with the operation team to supplement various popular games' preset combination keys so players could initialise the settings easily.
